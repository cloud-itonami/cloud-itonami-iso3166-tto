# Operator Guide

## First Deployment

1. Confirm the client's incorporation/legal-entity status is complete
   (route to `cloud-itonami-M6910` or local counsel first if not).
2. Register the client's intake: business type, target public function,
   prior filing history in Trinidad and Tobago if any, and whether the
   operator is (or is controlled by) a foreign investor under the
   Foreign Investment Act, Chap. 70:07.
3. Run the advisor in read-only mode against the Office of Procurement
   Regulation (OPR)'s Procurement Depository (oprtt.org), governed by
   the Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Property Act, 2015
   (Act No. 1 of 2015, as amended by Act No. 5 of 2016, Act No. 3 of
   2017, Act No. 27 of 2020 and Act No. 13 of 2023).
4. Compare the checklist against the client's current documentation
   (RGD Companies Registry Certificate of Incorporation, BIR File
   Number record from the Board of Inland Revenue, Procurement
   Depository supplier/contractor registration, and -- where the
   operator is a foreign investor -- confirmation that the applicable
   Foreign Investment Act obligation is met: a s.4 Minister notification
   on file for a private company, or a s.5(2) licence for a public
   company at or above 30% cumulative foreign shareholding).
5. Enable gated filing-draft assistance once the Market-Entry Compliance
   Governor contract is trusted; actual submission always requires human
   sign-off.

## Minimum Production Controls

- client-owned data store for business/tax registration documents
- clear provenance (official portal/regulation citation) for every
  requirement surfaced
- approval workflow for any portal registration or filing submission
- independent re-verification that the operator's declared Foreign
  Investment Act obligation (s.4 notification / s.5(2) licence) is
  actually met before any `:filing/submit` -- never trust a
  self-reported "compliant" claim
- named referral relationship with Trinidad-and-Tobago-licensed counsel
  or a registered agent for anything beyond checklist/draft assistance
- monthly audit export
- disputes/appeals over an OPR Ineligibility Proceedings sanction route
  to the OPR's own Hearing Panel review process and, on further review,
  the courts, as provided by the Public Procurement and Disposal of
  Public Property (Ineligibility Proceedings) Regulations, 2021, not
  this actor -- it has no standing to file a complaint on the client's
  behalf

## Certification

Certified operators must prove data provenance, audit traceability, that
automated actions cannot bypass the Market-Entry Compliance Governor, and
a working referral relationship with Trinidad-and-Tobago-licensed
counsel or a registered agent for whatever licensed representation the
law of Trinidad and Tobago requires for actual public-procurement
filings or Foreign Investment Act licence applications.
